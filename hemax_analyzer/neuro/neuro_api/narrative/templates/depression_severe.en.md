# Elevated risk of severe depressive episode
icon: 🌧️
severity: high
---
Your profile substantially exceeds the population average for severe depression risk ({{ p_depression_severe }}%, ×{{ ratio_depression_severe }}). This is **not a diagnosis** — diagnosis is made by a psychiatrist or therapist based on clinical interview, not lab values.

Severe depression is common but very treatable — psychotherapy plus, when indicated, medication produces significant improvement in most cases.
---
actions:
- **Schedule a primary care physician, psychiatrist, or therapist visit within 1-2 weeks**
- Psychotherapy (CBT has strong evidence)
- Medication if indicated by clinician
- Regular sleep schedule (same bedtime, 7-9 hours)
- Moderate exercise 150 min/week — effect comparable to mild antidepressant therapy
- Reduce alcohol — it worsens depression
- Track mood (PHQ-9 every 2 weeks online)
- Reach out to someone close to you — social support matters
---
red_flags:
- Thoughts of self-harm or suicide — **call 988 (US) or 112 / 911 / 7333 (UA)**
- Complete loss of interest in everyday activities > 2 weeks
- Unintentional weight loss > 5% in one month
- Severe insomnia or hypersomnia daily for > 2 weeks
- Psychotic symptoms (hallucinations, delusions) — **urgent psychiatric care**
