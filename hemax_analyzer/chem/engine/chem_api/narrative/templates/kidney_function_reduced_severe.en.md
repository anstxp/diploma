# Significantly reduced kidney function
icon: 🔴
severity: high
---
Your eGFR ({{ egfr }} mL/min/1.73m²) indicates **chronic kidney disease (CKD) stage III-IV**. Creatinine {{ creatinine }} mg/dL is also elevated. At this stage your kidneys have lost a significant portion of their filtering capacity.

This **does not mean dialysis is needed**, but the condition requires nephrology follow-up and medication adjustment. Preventing further progression is the priority.
---
actions:
- **See a nephrologist within 1-2 weeks**
- Get: repeat creatinine, urine protein, albumin/creatinine ratio, K, phosphate, calcium, PTH, vitamin D
- Check blood pressure (target <130/80)
- Diet with restricted salt and excess protein
- **Avoid NSAIDs** (ibuprofen, diclofenac) — they harm kidneys
- Review medications with your doctor — many need dose adjustment in CKD
---
red_flags:
- Leg or facial swelling
- Severe shortness of breath
- Reduced urine output
- Severe weakness, nausea
- Fever with chills — possible UTI
