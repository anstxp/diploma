# Elevated ferritin — possibly inflammatory
icon: 🦠
severity: low
---
Your ferritin is high but TSAT is normal or low. This **doesn't mean iron overload** — ferritin is also an **acute-phase protein**, meaning it rises during any inflammatory process (infections, autoimmune disease, obesity, liver disease).
---
actions:
- Get CRP — to evaluate inflammation
- Check liver enzymes (ALT, AST)
- See a GP — the inflammation cause needs identification
- If you have chronic conditions, discuss with your doctor
- If overweight, this can be a contributing factor
