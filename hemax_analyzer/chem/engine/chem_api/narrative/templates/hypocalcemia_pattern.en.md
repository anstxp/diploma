# Low calcium
icon: 🦴
severity: medium
---
Your calcium ({{ calcium }} mg/dL) is below normal. Hypocalcemia can cause muscle cramps, tingling, and in severe cases generalized seizures.

Possible causes:
- **Vitamin D deficiency** — most common
- **Hypoparathyroidism**
- **CKD**
- **Low albumin** — artifact (total calcium falsely low, ionized normal)
- **Certain medications** (bisphosphonates, anticonvulsants)
---
actions:
- See a doctor to identify the cause
- Get: albumin, vitamin D (25-OH), phosphate, magnesium, PTH
- If albumin is low — calcium may be falsely low; ionized calcium is more reliable
- Don't take large calcium doses without prescription
- Adequate dairy and dark leafy greens
---
red_flags:
- Tingling in lips or fingers
- Muscle cramps (especially in calves, hands)
- Palpitations
- Generalized seizures — **go to ER**
