# Critically high triglycerides — pancreatitis risk
icon: 🔴
severity: high
---
Your triglycerides ({{ trigly }} mg/dL) are **very high**. At values above 500 mg/dL (5.6 mmol/L), there's a real **risk of acute pancreatitis** — a life-threatening condition.

Causes: familial lipid disorders, uncontrolled diabetes, obesity, heavy alcohol use, certain medications.
---
actions:
- **See a doctor within 1-2 days**
- This result needs to be repeated fasting (≥12 hours) — to rule out postprandial elevation
- Get: HbA1c, TSH, full fasting lipid panel
- **Complete alcohol abstinence**
- Sharp restriction of fats and sweets until you see a doctor
- May need fibrates or high-dose omega-3 prescriptions
---
red_flags:
- **Severe abdominal pain (especially upper, radiating to back) — go to ER** — possible acute pancreatitis
- Nausea, vomiting
- Yellowish skin lumps (xanthomas)
- Milky-white blood serum (lab will notice)
