# Low sodium with high glucose
icon: ⚡
severity: medium
---
Your sodium ({{ sodium }} mmol/L) is low while **glucose is elevated ({{ glucose }} mg/dL)**. This is often **pseudohyponatremia**: high glucose pulls water into the bloodstream, diluting sodium. Glucose-corrected sodium often turns out close to normal.

True hyponatremia in this setting can occur too — but it needs different management than the "artifact" form. Your doctor will calculate corrected sodium.
---
actions:
- See a doctor, ideally an endocrinologist or GP
- Control glucose — sugar stabilization often normalizes sodium
- Get: HbA1c, repeat electrolytes, serum osmolality
- If you have diabetes, this signals weak glucose control
---
red_flags:
- Severe thirst + frequent urination + confusion — possible hyperosmolar state, **see a doctor immediately**
- Fruity breath — possible ketoacidosis
- Severe weakness or loss of consciousness
