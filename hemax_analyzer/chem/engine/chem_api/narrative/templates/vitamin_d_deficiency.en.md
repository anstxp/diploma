# Vitamin D deficiency
icon: ☀️
severity: low
---
Your vitamin D level ({{ vitd_25oh }} ng/mL) is low. **Vitamin D deficiency is very common** (~50-70% in northern latitudes, especially in winter). It affects immunity, bones, muscles, and mood.

Mild deficiency is often asymptomatic but can present as fatigue, perceived tiredness, muscle weakness, frequent infections.
---
actions:
- See a GP for assessment and dosing
- Standard deficiency dose: **2000-5000 IU/day** for 2-3 months, then 1000-2000 IU maintenance
- Don't take very high doses without medical advice — vitamin D overdose is harmful
- More sun exposure (15-30 min/day, face and arms uncovered)
- Include fatty fish, eggs, mushrooms in your diet
- Recheck level in 3 months
---
red_flags:
- Severe weakness, difficulty rising from a chair
- Bone pain
- Frequent fractures
- Vitamin D deficiency + low calcium or high PTH — needs deeper workup
