# Very high LDL — possible familial hypercholesterolemia
icon: 💗
severity: medium
---
Your LDL cholesterol ({{ ldl }} mg/dL) is very high. Values ≥190 mg/dL (4.9 mmol/L) often indicate **familial hypercholesterolemia (FH)** — an inherited cholesterol-metabolism disorder affecting 1 in 250 people.

FH carries high risk of premature cardiovascular events (early heart attack, stroke) if untreated. The good news: effective treatment exists — statins reduce risk severalfold.
---
actions:
- **See a cardiologist or GP**
- Get: repeat fasting lipid panel; for refinement — LDL particles (LDL-P), Lp(a)
- Check family history — early heart attack/stroke in close relatives (men <55, women <65)?
- Standard cardiovascular risk workup: ECG, possibly CT calcium scoring
- Siblings/children should also have lipids checked
- Early statin therapy significantly improves prognosis
---
red_flags:
- Chest pain on exertion — possible angina
- Yellowish lumps on tendons (xanthomas)
- Family history of early heart attacks — strong additional FH argument
