# Possible Gilbert's syndrome
icon: 🧬
severity: low
---
Your total bilirubin ({{ bilirubin_total }} mg/dL) is elevated but liver enzymes (ALT, AST, ALP) are normal. This **isolated unconjugated hyperbilirubinemia** is characteristic of **Gilbert's syndrome** — a common (3-7% of people) **inherited trait**, not a disease.

In Gilbert's, the liver clears unconjugated bilirubin slightly less efficiently, especially during stress, fasting, or infection. It **doesn't harm health**, requires no treatment, and doesn't affect lifespan.
---
actions:
- See a GP to rule out other causes (viral hepatitis, hemolysis)
- Get: fractionated bilirubin (direct + indirect), HBsAg, anti-HCV, reticulocytes
- If confirmed, no specific action needed
- Avoid prolonged fasting and excessive exercise — they worsen it
- Some drugs are tolerated less well in Gilbert's — discuss with your doctor
---
red_flags:
- Jaundice + right upper abdominal pain or hard liver — needs further workup
- Dark urine or pale stools
- Jaundice + worsening weakness
