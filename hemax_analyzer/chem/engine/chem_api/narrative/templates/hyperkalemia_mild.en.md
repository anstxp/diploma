# Mildly elevated potassium
icon: ⚡
severity: medium
---
Your potassium ({{ potassium }} mmol/L) is above normal. Mild hyperkalemia is often asymptomatic but should be investigated.

Common causes:
- **CKD** — even at early stages
- **Medications** — ACEi, ARBs, spironolactone, NSAIDs, heparin
- **Hemolyzed sample** — lab artifact, requires retesting
- High K-rich food intake with reduced kidney function
---
actions:
- Repeat test within a week — hemolyzed samples give falsely high K
- See a GP — kidney function should be checked
- Get: creatinine, eGFR
- Review medications with your doctor
- If on ACEi/ARB/spironolactone, don't stop without consulting your doctor — but inform them
- Temporarily limit bananas, potatoes, tomatoes, salt substitutes
---
red_flags:
- Palpitations, irregular heartbeat
- Muscle weakness
- Tingling
