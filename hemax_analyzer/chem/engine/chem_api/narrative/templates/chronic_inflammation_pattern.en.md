# Mildly elevated CRP
icon: 🦠
severity: low
---
Your CRP ({{ crp }} mg/L) is mildly elevated. At 5-30 mg/L, this is often related to:

- Chronic conditions: obesity, metabolic syndrome, cardiovascular disease
- Minor or viral infection
- Autoimmune conditions (RA, SLE, etc.)
- Stress, poor sleep
- Smoking
---
actions:
- See a GP for context
- Get: CBC, ESR, creatinine, liver tests
- Check cardiovascular factors (BP, lipids, glucose)
- If overweight, weight loss often reduces CRP
- Regular exercise, smoking cessation — significantly reduce chronic inflammation
---
red_flags:
- Persistent fever without cause
- Unexplained joint pain, rash
- Weight loss, night sweats
