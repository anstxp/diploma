# Mildly low blood sugar
icon: 🩸
severity: low
---
Your glucose ({{ glucose }} mg/dL) is below the normal range. **Mild hypoglycemia** can have various causes: missed meals, intense exercise, certain medications (especially antidiabetic), reactive hypoglycemia after eating.

If this is a **single asymptomatic event**, it's likely not serious. If it recurs, the cause should be investigated.
---
actions:
- If you haven't eaten yet, have a balanced meal with protein and complex carbs
- Repeat fasting test in 1-2 weeks
- If recurrent, get: HbA1c, fasting insulin, C-peptide
- Track when it happens — fasting, post-meal, after exercise?
- See your GP with notes
---
red_flags:
- Frequent episodes (several per week)
- Symptoms: shaking, palpitations, sweating, hunger between meals
- Hypoglycemia after eating (possible reactive form)
