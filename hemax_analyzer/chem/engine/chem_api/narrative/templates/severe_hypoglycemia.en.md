# Critically low blood sugar
icon: 🔴
severity: high
---
Your glucose ({{ glucose }} mg/dL) is significantly below safe levels. **Hypoglycemia** at this severity requires **immediate action** — values below 55 mg/dL (3.0 mmol/L) can cause confusion, loss of consciousness, or seizures.

If this is fasting, the cause needs to be investigated urgently (possible insulinoma, reactive hypoglycemia, drug effects). If you take insulin or other antidiabetic medications, this may be an overdose.
---
actions:
- **If symptoms (shaking, sweating, confusion) — eat 15-20 g of fast carbs immediately** (juice, candy, honey)
- Recheck in 15 minutes; repeat carbs if still low
- **See a doctor the same day** — the cause must be identified
- If on diabetes medications, discuss dosing with your doctor
- Track when and under what circumstances hypoglycemia occurs
---
red_flags:
- Loss of consciousness, seizures, inability to swallow
- Confusion, disorientation
- Heavy shaking + cold sweat + flushing
- Hypoglycemia without clear cause or after exercise — see a doctor immediately
