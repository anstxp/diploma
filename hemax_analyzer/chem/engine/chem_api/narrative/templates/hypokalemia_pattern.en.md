# Low potassium
icon: ⚡
severity: medium
---
Your potassium ({{ potassium }} mmol/L) is below normal. Hypokalemia can cause muscle weakness, cramps, and arrhythmias.

Common causes:
- **Diuretic use** (furosemide, thiazides) — most common
- **GI losses** — diarrhea, vomiting
- **Metabolic alkalosis** (especially from vomiting)
- **Hyperaldosteronism** — rarer cause
- Magnesium deficiency — often coexists
---
actions:
- See a doctor — especially if on diuretics
- Get: repeat potassium, magnesium, creatinine, bicarbonate
- Increase K-rich foods: bananas, potatoes, tomatoes, spinach, avocado
- If on diuretics, discuss K-sparing options or K supplementation with your doctor
- Don't self-supplement potassium tablets
---
red_flags:
- Muscle weakness, especially in legs
- Palpitations, arrhythmia
- Severe cramps
- Severe hypokalemia (<2.5) — see a doctor urgently
