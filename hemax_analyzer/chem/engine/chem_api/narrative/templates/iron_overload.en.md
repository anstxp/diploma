# Iron overload
icon: 🟠
severity: medium
---
Your ferritin ({{ ferritin }} ng/mL) is high with elevated TSAT ({{ tsat }}%). This combination indicates **iron overload**, most commonly from **hereditary hemochromatosis** (HH) — an inherited condition where the body absorbs too much iron.

Untreated, excess iron accumulates in liver, heart, pancreas, and skin, gradually damaging them. The good news: treatment is effective (regular phlebotomy).
---
actions:
- **See a hematologist or hepatologist**
- Get: repeat ferritin and TSAT, genetic test for HFE mutations (C282Y, H63D), ALT/AST
- Liver ultrasound for structural assessment
- Avoid iron supplements and high-dose vitamin C
- Limit red meat
- Avoid raw seafood
- Minimal alcohol (worsens liver damage)
---
red_flags:
- Right upper abdominal pain or heaviness
- "Bronze" skin pigmentation
- Joint pain (especially 2nd-3rd MCP joints)
- Onset of diabetes
- Palpitations, signs of heart failure
