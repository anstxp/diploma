# Elevated calcium
icon: 🦴
severity: medium
---
Your calcium ({{ calcium }} mg/dL) is above normal. Hypercalcemia requires investigation — most commonly **primary hyperparathyroidism** or malignancy.

Other causes:
- Vitamin D or calcium overdose
- Prolonged immobilization
- Sarcoidosis
- Thiazide diuretics
- Familial hypercalcemias
---
actions:
- See a doctor — cause must be identified
- Get: PTH (parathyroid hormone), vitamin D (25-OH), phosphate, creatinine, 24-hr urine calcium
- Drink adequate water
- Don't take calcium or vitamin D without prescription
- If on thiazide diuretics, discuss with your doctor
---
red_flags:
- "Stones, bones, abdominal groans, psychic moans" — kidney stones, bone pain, abdominal pain, confusion
- Severe thirst + frequent urination
- Constipation, nausea, vomiting
- Values >14 mg/dL — **go to ER**
