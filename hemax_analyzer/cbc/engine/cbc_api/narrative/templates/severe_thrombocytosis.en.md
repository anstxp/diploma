# Markedly elevated platelets
icon: 🟠
severity: medium
---
Your platelet count ({{ plt }} ×10⁹/L) is significantly above normal. At this level the cause needs to be identified — possibilities include:

- **Reactive thrombocytosis** — a response to something else: recent surgery (especially spleen removal), major trauma, acute infection, chronic inflammation, iron deficiency
- **Essential thrombocythemia** — a bone-marrow disorder in its own right, more common after 50-60
- After spleen removal, platelets may stay permanently elevated — this doesn't require treatment if no other issues

At levels above 600 ×10⁹/L there's a small thrombosis risk, so this shouldn't be ignored.
---
actions:
- See a GP for clinical evaluation — the reactive cause needs to be identified
- Discuss with your doctor: recent surgeries/trauma/infections, any sudden leg pain, chest pain, severe headaches
- Possible haematologist referral to rule out essential thrombocythemia (**JAK2**, **CALR** mutation testing)
- Repeat CBC in 4-6 weeks
---
red_flags:
- Sudden severe leg pain or swelling — possible deep vein thrombosis
- Sudden shortness of breath, chest pain — possible pulmonary embolism
- Severe headache, vision disturbance, numbness or weakness in limbs — possible stroke
