# Possible thalassemia trait (carrier)
icon: 🧬
severity: low
---
Your red cells are smaller than normal (MCV = {{ mcv }} fL), but you have **more of them than usual**, and your haemoglobin is either at the lower limit or normal. This is a classic picture of **alpha- or beta-thalassemia trait** — an inherited trait, not a disease.

Unlike iron deficiency, in thalassemia the body produces **more** small cells to compensate for their reduced size. Thalassemia is common in Mediterranean, Asian, Middle Eastern, and African populations.

Carriers usually **have no symptoms** and live normal lives. It's important to know your status — especially when planning children with a partner who might also be a carrier.
---
actions:
- Get **haemoglobin electrophoresis** — the main confirmatory test
- Also check **ferritin** — to rule out coexisting iron deficiency
- See a GP or haematologist for interpretation
- If planning a family, discuss having your partner tested too
- **Do not take iron supplements without a prescription** — without deficiency, iron can cause harm in thalassemia
---
red_flags:
- If follow-up shows a form more severe than minor — planned follow-up with a haematologist
