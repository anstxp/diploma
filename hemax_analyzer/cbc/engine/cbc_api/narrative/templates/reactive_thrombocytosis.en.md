# Mildly elevated platelets
icon: 🔹
severity: low
---
Your platelet count ({{ plt }} ×10⁹/L) is above normal. In the vast majority of cases this is **reactive (secondary) thrombocytosis** — a response to something else, not a disease in itself.

Typical causes: recent infection, inflammation, iron deficiency, trauma or surgery, pregnancy, certain medications, stress response.

If this finding accompanies others (e.g. iron deficiency or infection pattern) — that's the explanation, and we've described it in the main story above.
---
actions:
- If there's an obvious cause (infection, trauma, iron therapy) — reactive thrombocytosis usually resolves 1-2 weeks after the trigger clears
- Repeat the blood test in 4-6 weeks to confirm normalization
- If still elevated on repeat, discuss with your GP
---
red_flags:
- Unbearable headache or vision disturbance
- Numbness or weakness in limbs (possible signs of thrombosis)
- Pain and swelling in a leg (suspected deep vein thrombosis)
