# Pattern of active infection
icon: 🦠
severity: medium
---
Your white cell count is elevated ({{ wbc }} ×10⁹/L), mostly due to **neutrophils** — the type of white cell that responds first to bacterial infections. This combination most often suggests:

- **Acute bacterial infection** (most common cause)
- Less likely — acute non-infectious inflammation (trauma, heart attack, surgery)
- Rarely — stress response or heavy physical exertion

{% if signals.nlr_high %}
Your **NLR index** (neutrophil-to-lymphocyte ratio) is also elevated — an additional marker of active inflammation that helps distinguish bacterial from viral infections.
{% endif %}
---
actions:
- If you have symptoms (fever, pain, fatigue, cough, etc.) — **see a doctor today or tomorrow**
- If you have no symptoms, still show these results to a doctor soon to investigate
- Drink plenty of fluids and rest
- Do not start antibiotics without a prescription — not every infection needs them
---
red_flags:
- High fever (above 38.5°C / 101.3°F) that doesn't respond to fever reducers
- Severe headache + stiff neck
- Severe shortness of breath or chest pain
- Confusion, extreme weakness
- Progressive worsening
