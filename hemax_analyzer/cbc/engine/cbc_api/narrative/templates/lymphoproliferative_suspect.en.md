# Markedly elevated lymphocytes — haematologist consultation recommended
icon: ⚠️
severity: medium
---
Your total white cell count is elevated ({{ wbc }} ×10⁹/L), predominantly due to **lymphocytes** ({{ alc }} ×10⁹/L). At your age ({{ age }} years), this pattern often requires ruling out lymphoproliferative disorders, particularly **chronic lymphocytic leukemia (CLL)**.

Important: CLL is a **chronic, usually slowly progressing disease**. In many patients it requires only observation, not treatment, for years. But confirmation requires specialized testing.

Less likely causes of high lymphocytes at your age: viral infections, stress, reactive states.
---
actions:
- See your GP — they'll refer you to a **haematologist**
- The haematologist will order: repeat CBC with differential, **lymphocyte immunophenotyping (flow cytometry)**, ultrasound of lymph nodes and abdomen
- Before the visit, note: any enlarged lymph nodes on neck/armpits/groin, night sweats, unexplained weight loss
- Not urgent, but don't delay — ideally within 2-4 weeks
---
red_flags:
- Rapid lymph node enlargement
- Heavy night sweats
- Unexplained weight loss (>10% in 6 months)
- Recurrent infections
