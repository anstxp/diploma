# Anaemia with normal-sized red cells
icon: 🩸
severity: medium
---
Your haemoglobin is low, but the size of your red cells is normal (MCV = {{ mcv }} fL). This pattern — normocytic anaemia — doesn't point to one specific cause, but is often seen in:

- **Chronic diseases** (inflammatory conditions, chronic kidney disease, liver disease)
- **Early iron deficiency** (before red cells have had time to shrink)
- **Anaemia after acute blood loss**
- **Haemolysis** (accelerated red-cell destruction)

{% if age and age >= 60 %}
In people over 60, normocytic anaemia is often related to chronic kidney disease or chronic inflammatory processes.
{% endif %}
---
actions:
- Get tested: **ferritin**, iron, transferrin; **creatinine + eGFR** for kidney function; **C-reactive protein** for inflammation; **reticulocytes** for regeneration
- See a general practitioner — a full history and exam will help narrow the cause
- Bring a list of chronic conditions and medications
---
red_flags:
- Significant shortness of breath at rest, or chest pain
- Dark urine or yellowing of skin (may indicate haemolysis)
- Leg swelling, breathlessness on exertion (may indicate kidney issues)
