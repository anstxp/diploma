# Possible dehydration
icon: 💧
severity: low
---
Your haematocrit ({{ hct }}%) and haemoglobin are elevated, but your red cell count is normal. This is a classic picture of **haemoconcentration** — a state where you have the usual number of red cells but less plasma (the liquid part of blood). In other words, your blood is slightly concentrated.

Most common cause: not drinking enough water before the test, heavy sweating, diarrhea, fever. Also — prolonged hot weather exposure.

This is **not a disease**, but rather a temporary state that's easy to fix.
---
actions:
- Increase water intake over the next few days (1.5-2 L/day for an average adult)
- Repeat the test in 1-2 weeks after staying well-hydrated the day before
- If still elevated, discuss with your GP
---
red_flags:
- Significant weakness, thirst, infrequent urination, dark urine
- Dizziness when standing
